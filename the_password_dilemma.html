The Password Dilemma

In the picture above, I have addressed a common problem that almost every person who has ever used authentication has faced. I have named it “The Password Dilemma.” My current focus is to work on this widespread issue and develop a user-friendly solution to assist people in the easiest and most effective way.

This picture is taken from my latest unpublished paper, which I have released on arXiv, where I have integrated cybersecurity, machine learning, and human–computer interaction into a single framework. My next goal in this project is to create an environment that will allow me to conduct surveys to develop legitimate user profiles.

The three novel solutions I have proposed so far—Password Dissection Mechanism, Dynamic Password Policy Mechanism, and AdaptAuth—are still in their theoretical stages. The empirical studies I plan to conduct on these mechanisms would likely take months or even years, requiring continuous and consistent participation to properly understand user behavior. In my opinion, there is no better place than a university setting to carry out such work.

However, as a self-employed researcher, that goal is currently on hold, and I need the collaboration of like-minded scholars to advance these works further. Expert insights are essential for designing and developing the environment required for these empirical studies.

Because of its user-profile-based identification approach, this work also has the potential to provide solutions in other domains where authentication is essential. Moreover, as I discussed in my previous post, it offers anti-theft functionalities and the potential to create a centralized authentication mechanism capable of understanding users’ password behavior and accurately detecting illegitimate activities such as unauthorized logins and access attempts. I also believe this framework could pave the way toward password-less authentication in the future.
