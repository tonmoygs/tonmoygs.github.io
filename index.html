About Me

I'm Tonmoy Ghosh, a cybersecurity enthusiast with aspirations to advance as a researcher and contribute to strengthening public and private security through innovative approaches.

I'm a cybersecurity researcher and Certified Ethical Hacker (CEH) from Dhaka, Bangladesh. I hold a B.Sc. in Software Engineering from Daffodil International University (CGPA 3.80/4.00), where my thesis focused on SQL injectability detection using deep learning.
My research interest lies at the intersection of Cybersecurity, Human-Computer Interaction, and Machine Learning. I have proposed several innovative solutions in password security that take into account extensive human behavioral patterns, system data, and network characteristics. With that, I am aiming to identify people individually and also aiming to build a centralized password security framework. My research in password security lays the foundation for futuristic password-less authentication mechanism. Earlier, I have worked with the Cyber Security Center, Daffodil International University (DIU) and participated in the CyberVSR Program at the Rochester Institute of Technology, contributing to projects involving threat mapping and cyber resilience analysis.
I aim to become a Computer Security Scientist, developing intelligent, human-centric, and resilient defense frameworks to strengthen global cybersecurity.
